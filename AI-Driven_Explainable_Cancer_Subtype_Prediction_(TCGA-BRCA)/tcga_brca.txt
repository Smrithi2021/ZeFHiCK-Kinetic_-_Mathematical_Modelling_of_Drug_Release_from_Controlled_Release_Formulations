The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) BRCA cohort consists of over 1,000 breast
cancer samples with multi-omics profiling, including RNA sequencing,
mutation data, copy number variation, and clinical metadata.

TCGA-BRCA data has been extensively used to study molecular subtypes,
tumor heterogeneity, and biomarker discovery. RNA-seq data enables
expression-based classification such as PAM50.

The dataset provides a standardized reference for developing and validating
machine learning models in oncology research.

TCGA findings have contributed significantly to understanding breast cancer
biology and improving patient stratification.
