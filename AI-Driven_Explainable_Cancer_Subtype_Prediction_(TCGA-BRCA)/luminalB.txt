Luminal B breast cancer shares estrogen receptor positivity with Luminal A
but is characterized by higher proliferation and poorer prognosis.
These tumors often show increased expression of cell cycle–related genes.

Key genes linked to Luminal B subtype include ESR1, CCNB1, MYC, and MKI67.
Compared to Luminal A, Luminal B tumors exhibit higher grade and increased
risk of recurrence.

TCGA analyses indicate that Luminal B tumors show partial overlap with
HER2 signaling and proliferative pathways, making them biologically more
aggressive.

Treatment strategies often include both endocrine therapy and chemotherapy.
