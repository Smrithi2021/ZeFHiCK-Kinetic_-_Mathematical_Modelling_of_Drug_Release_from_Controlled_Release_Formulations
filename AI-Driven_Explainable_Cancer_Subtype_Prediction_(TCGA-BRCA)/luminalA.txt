Luminal A breast cancer is characterized by strong estrogen receptor (ER)
expression, low proliferation rates, and relatively favorable prognosis.
These tumors typically respond well to endocrine therapy.

Genes commonly associated with Luminal A subtype include ESR1, PGR, GATA3,
and FOXA1. Expression of proliferation-related genes such as MKI67 is
generally low compared to other subtypes.

TCGA studies have shown that Luminal A tumors cluster distinctly based on
transcriptomic profiles reflecting hormone-driven growth rather than
growth factor signaling.

Clinically, Luminal A tumors are associated with longer overall survival
and lower recurrence rates.
