PAM50 is a gene expression–based classification system used to categorize
breast cancer tumors into intrinsic molecular subtypes. It is derived from
the expression patterns of 50 genes related to hormone signaling, cell
proliferation, and growth factor pathways.

The five PAM50 subtypes are Luminal A, Luminal B, HER2-enriched, Basal-like,
and Normal-like. These subtypes differ in prognosis, response to therapy,
and underlying tumor biology.

Luminal subtypes are typically estrogen receptor positive and associated
with better outcomes, while Basal-like tumors are often triple-negative
and more aggressive. HER2-enriched tumors show activation of ERBB2-related
signaling pathways.

PAM50 classification is widely used in TCGA studies and clinical research
to stratify patients and guide therapeutic decisions.
