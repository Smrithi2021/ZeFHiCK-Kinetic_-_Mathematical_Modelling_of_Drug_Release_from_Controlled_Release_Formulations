Basal-like breast cancer is an aggressive subtype often overlapping with
triple-negative breast cancer, lacking expression of ER, PR, and HER2.

These tumors show high expression of basal cytokeratins such as KRT5 and
KRT14, along with proliferation-related genes like MKI67 and cell cycle
regulators.

TCGA studies reveal that Basal-like tumors are genomically unstable and
associated with poor prognosis and limited targeted treatment options.

Chemotherapy remains the primary treatment, and research is ongoing to
identify novel therapeutic targets for this subtype.
